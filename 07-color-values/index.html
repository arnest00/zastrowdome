About Panagia Apsinthiotissa

Panagia Apsinthiotissa is a Greek Orthodox monastery located at the southern foot of the Pentadaktylos range in the Republic of Cyprus. It was established in the eleventh or twelfth century.

According to a local legend, the monastery was named after a wormwood bush that covered the mouth of the cave in which a monk had hidden a religiou icon in order to save it during the period of Byzantine Iconoclasm.
