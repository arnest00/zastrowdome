The Most Brightest Stars

Excluding the sun, the brightest stars in Earth's sky, arranged by apparent magnitude, are Sirius, Canopus, the binary star Alpha Centauri AB (comprised of Rigil Kentaurus and Toliman), Arcturus, and Vega. A star's apparent magnitude, or its brightness as observed from Earth, differs from its absolute magnitude, or its intrinsic stellar luminosity. One of the main factors that a star is bright is its proximity to our planet, and does not necessarily mean it is also one of the most luminous stars in the universe.
